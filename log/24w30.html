Test 10

Here the idea is to run the rotation for many different values of magnetic field.

There are two direct observations

  1. We can quantitatively see what we kind of saw before. The vertices hierarchy changes with different magnetic field values.
  2. The system is pretty much ordered according to .

I'm not sure what to make of all this.
It's curious that it doesn't follow the usual rules of a mean field theory, i.e. where is some coupled vertex type. But the more I think about it, it starts to make sense.
What we have seen so far is that this behavior only appears if you do the rotation "process", and increase the field many times, but that process has to happen. If it doesn't happen we go back to the usual behaviour.
I feel this makes sense, because in the end, while increasing the magnitude, the direction/motion of the field is not trivial.
In the static case it goes back to a mean field theory, where the quenching in a fixed direction works. Previously we saw that in and reproduce 2in/out and the 4in/4out + spin freezing case.
However in the dynamic case it does not. It would be as if we think of temperature in thermodynamics as a vector and not a scalar... I guess?

At the same time I don't know what the description of all this would be.
So a bit my hasty takeaway, is that the magnitude of the field is not mappable to an equivalent of the temperature in non-static cases.
