In-trap motion of particles

I wanted to see how the particles move as the angle rotates. To do this I measured the distance traveled from their initial state. This is particular helpful since all particles start from the equilibrium position.

normalized with the trap separation. This will tell us if the particles travel the whole trap, or get stuck in the central hill.

Where I averaged across all particles and across all realizations. I also included values up to where all de interesting dynamics occur.
From here we can see that for fast rotations, the horizontal particles move from one side to the other of the trap, while the verticals move a bit, and go back to the equilibrium position.

The interesting case comes from the slow rotations. We can see that that both move a lot and vibrate up to 20 degrees, then the horizontals start to move to the other side of the trap, slowly but surely, while the verticals do not really decide what to do. Then around 40 degrees the verticals go all over the place but in average they move a lot around the central hill.

What I want to know is what happens with the forces around 20 and 40 degrees.

The standard deviation of the vertical traps are all over the place..
